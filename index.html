Why studs?

Once you’ve used studs, wheel bolts feel ancient.

If you’ve ever balanced a wheel on your knee while trying to line up bolt holes, you already know why stud conversions exist. This just makes it official.

Real-world benefits

  • Faster, easier wheel changes in the driveway or pits.
  • Less chance of cross-threading wheel bolts in a rush.
  • Helps keep spacers and wheels located while you torque nuts down.
  • Cleaner look, especially with open nuts and track wheels.

Designed around track use

These kits are being developed around the needs of owners who actually drive their cars hard – not purely for show. The idea is simple: take the hassle out of swapping between street and track wheel setups, while maintaining confidence in the hardware holding everything together.

All recommendations assume proper installation and torque using quality tools. If in doubt, have a trusted workshop install and check everything.

Install & fitment

Straightforward install, no drama.

Stud conversions are a simple job for a competent DIY owner or workshop. Always use common sense, quality tools and proper torque practices.

Basic process

  • Remove factory wheel bolts and wheels.
  • Clean hub threads and check for damage or corrosion.
  • Install studs with the recommended thread locker if required.
  • Torque to spec, allow thread locker to cure before driving.
  • Refit wheels and torque nuts in a star pattern.

Good practices

  • Always torque nuts with a calibrated torque wrench.
  • Re-check torque after the first short drive / heat cycle.
  • Inspect studs and nuts as part of your regular service routine.
  • If unsure at any point, get a professional to install and check.

Important note

All motorsport-style hardware should be installed and used at your own risk.

E9X AutoHaus products are designed with care, but cannot account for every possible combination of wheels, spacers, tyres, and driving styles. Always verify clearances, torque values and local regulations before use.
